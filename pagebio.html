Mayor Randy Hope

Mayor of Municipality of Chatham-Kent


On behalf of Council, staff and citizens of the Municipality of Chatham-Kent, I welcome all delegates, vendors and speakers participating in the 2013 MISA Security Conference! The Municipality of Chatham-Kent recognizes the role and importance of information technology as an engine for global economic and social development. Information technology investments are an increasingly important area of focus for elected officials and administrative leadership. While in Chatham-Kent, I hope you have the opportunity to explore our green, progressive and friendly community, which offers the best of urban and rural life for people of all ages...

Randy Hope Mayor

Helen Mclaren

Director of Information Technology, Municipality of Chatham-Kent


Welcome to the Municipality of Chatham-Kent and the 2013 MISA Security Conference: Application Security – There’s a Patch for That! The conference team has worked hard to put together a program that we hope you find interesting, entertaining and educational. The three keynote sessions and nine breakout sessions offered in three tracks - General, Technical and Management - provides opportunities to learn the latest in application security trends and practices. The schedule of events, activities and entertainment will hopefully facilitate peer networking, discussions, friendships and most of all, fun! (You may even witness a pirate or two stalking the halls!) The combined abilities and efforts of our ITS team, along with support from the MISA Executive have provided for a conference that I hope you all enjoy, which would not be possible without the generous support of our vendors and sponsors! Thank you for joining us in Chatham-Kent!
With best wishes,

Helen Mclaren Director of Information Technology, Municipality of Chatham-Kent

Topic: Ordered Chaos: Managing the Security Pedigree of the Corporate Application Portfolio

Speaker: Murray Rosenthal – City of Toronto

Date/Time: Wednesday Oct 23 3:30PM

Murray is a well-respected security practitioner who has worked for both private and public sector organizations over the past 20+ years.  He is a sought after speaker, having addressed security forums across North America on topical issues including PCI, the ISO 27000 series, Gov 2.0, social media, secure software manufacture and security architecture.  Murray is published in the proceedings of the American Society for Quality Control, and has written for, and been interviewed by, Municipal Interface.  He sits on the Program Advisory Committee, School of Applied Computing and Engineering Sciences, Sheridan Institute of Technology and Advanced Learning, and collaborated on the 2011 SABSA TOGAF white paper integration initiative, led by The Open Group.  Murray is the municipal government representative on the Identity Management Subcommittee Working Group operating under the ægis of the Joint Councils.  His two articles on security related matters affecting intelligent, web based agents in the digital trust context appear in the book, SmartData: Privacy Meets Evolutionary Robotics, published earlier this year by Springer.
