"The 5 Years of TELUS - Rotman Management School Study on Best Security Practices for Canadian Organizations"
Wednesday Oct 24 | 3:30 PM â€“ 4:30 PM | Room 1A - General Track

Every year, TELUS partners with The Rotman Management School U of T and performs an independent study on best security practices for Canadian organizations. It is the only Canadian based security report available to organizations in Canada today. We will review the last 4 years of results and compare and contrast that to the findings of this years qualitative report. The TELUS/Rotman study offers:

  • Original, Canadian research, based on the perspectives of government, public and private sector organizations
  • Findings on emerging trends and technologies, including breaches, social networking, compliance risk, BYOD, and mobile security
  • Points of view spanning the entire security organization from the executive to the IT manager-level.

This year's study offers insights on:

  • Has my organization been breached and I don't know about it?
  • How will a breach affect my brand?
  • What are my employees doing with corporate data?
  • How do I retain my best IT security employees?

Karen Ellis is a 25 year veteran of information technology and IT security, leading large international teams. Karen's background includes managing international contact centre's global network teams, IT delivery teams, sales organizations and product development teams. As the Director for TELUS Security Solutions, Karen is focused on the Public Sector working with Government across all levels - federally, provincially and locally.
